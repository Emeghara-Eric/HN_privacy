# HealthNode Privacy Policy  
**Last Updated:** [Insert Date]

---

## 1. Introduction  
HealthNode is a personal women’s health companion designed to help users track menstrual cycles, predict ovulation, and log symptoms.  
This Privacy Policy explains how HealthNode collects, uses, and protects your information.

---

## 2. Data Collection and Usage (Version 1.0.0)  
The current version of HealthNode does **not** require users to create an account, sign in, or provide personal information.

All data is stored **locally on your device**, including:

- Period logs  
- Cycle dates  
- Symptoms  
- Predictions & insights  

No data is transmitted to any server or cloud service.

---

## 3. No Personal Data Collection  
HealthNode does **not** collect or process:

- Names or contact details  
- Location information  
- Device identifiers  
- Financial information  
- Photos, media, or files  
- Health data outside what you manually enter  

The data you enter is used solely to provide app functionality on your device.

---

## 4. No Data Sharing or Selling  
HealthNode does **not**:

- Share data with third parties  
- Sell user data  
- Use data for advertising  
- Use third-party analytics  

Your information remains entirely on your device.

---

## 5. Permissions Used  
The MVP version of HealthNode does **not** request sensitive permissions.  
If future versions require additional permissions (such as internet for login), they will be clearly disclosed.

---

## 6. Data Security  
All data is stored locally.  
Security of your data depends on your device's storage and security settings.

Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all locally stored data.

---

## 7. Future Updates (Cloud Storage & Login)  
Future versions of HealthNode may include:

- Optional user accounts  
- Secure cloud backup  
- Log-in/authentication features  

If these features are added, this Privacy Policy will be updated.  
User privacy will remain a top priority.

---

## 8. Children’s Privacy  
HealthNode is **not** intended for children under 13.  
We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.
